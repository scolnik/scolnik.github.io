Cats vs Dogs

Attempting to solve the age-old rivalry by analyzing Reddit comments using Natural Language Processing

Using Reddit and Pushshift.io APIs, I collected comments from two different subreddits (r/cats and r/dogs). Preliminary EDA showed that of the 30 most frequent words in each subreddit, approximately 1/3 were unique to the class and 2/3 were the same in both.

CountVectorizer and TfidfVectorizer (NLP modules from scikit-learn) were each tested for vectorizing the text data. After preprocessing and vectorizing the text data, I trained various classification models to identify which subreddit a given post came from. Logistic regression, Random forest, and Multinomial naive Bayes models were trained and tested with Gridsearch. The final model used CountVectorizer and LogisticRegression to achieve 84.8% accuracy (compared to the baseline accuracy of 50% for balanced classes).

Sentiment analysis found that the comments in /r/cats were generally more positive than comments in /r/dogs, however I cannot conclude whether cats or dogs are superior. More research (and cute pet photos) are needed.

Skills and Tools Used:
  • Webscraping and APIs for data gathering
  • Natural Language Processing: Scikit-learn modules CountVectorizer, TfidfVectorizer
  • Machine learning models: Scikit-learn LogisticRegression, MultinomialNB, RandomForestClassifier
  • Training and testing models: Scikit-learn modules Pipeline, GridSearchCV, train_test_split

Aerial Image Analysis

Helping hurricane preparedness using drone images and a convolutional neural network

I worked with a team of classmates on a real-world project for the World Bank. Our project goal was to use aerial image data taken by drone to identify buildings in need of repairs, to prepare for future hurricanes. Given that there are approximately 80,000 buildings on the island of St. Lucia, developing a model that can quickly screen for damaged roofs would be useful for anyone assessing how to prepare the island for hurricane season.

We were provided with a large aerial image, taken by drone, of the town of Dennery, St. Lucia. Individual roof images were cropped by layering with a GIS shapefile of building locations. We then trained a CNN (convolutional neural network) to correctly classify images as either non-roof, good-quality roof, or poor-quality roof. Our model correctly identified 38% of the non-roof images and 73% of the poor roof images in the dataset, with a total accuracy for all three categories of 72% from a 56% baseline. This is a strong proof of concept for the model's viability if it could be trained on a larger set of images.

Skills and Tools Used:
  • Geospatial data: ArcGIS, Rasterio, Geopandas
  • Neural network modeling: Keras (Python deep learning library)
  • Image transformation: Keras ImageDataGenerator
